## Summary
Company note for SanDisk: a leading brand in NAND flash-based storage products (removable media, embedded flash, and consumer SSDs). SanDisk's product portfolio targets consumer devices, mobile and embedded systems, and certain client storage markets; the brand and many product lines are now part of Western Digital.## Overview
SanDisk originally built its reputation on removable flash memory (SD cards, USB drives) and later expanded into embedded eMMC/UFS and consumer SSDs. The brand is widely licensed and recognized for consumer storage reliability and portability. After corporate transactions, SanDisk operates as a key flash-storage brand within Western Digital's broader storage portfolio.

## History & ownership
- Founded in 1988 (as SunDisk) and later renamed SanDisk, the company grew through NAND innovation and consumer products.
- Acquired by Western Digital in 2016; many SanDisk product lines and IP are now managed under Western Digital's flash business.

## Products & technologies
- Removable flash memory: SD cards, microSD, USB flash drives (SanDisk consumer lineup).
- Embedded flash: eMMC and UFS for mobile devices and embedded systems (SanDisk-branded OEM parts and white-label solutions).
- Client SSDs: SATA and NVMe SSDs for laptops, desktops and external drives (SanDisk-branded and OEM models).
- NAND technology: SanDisk historically invested in NAND development and design (now part of WD's joint NAND supply arrangements).

## Markets & customers
- Consumer electronics (camera storage, mobile devices, USB storage).
- OEMs requiring embedded flash for smartphones, tablets, and IoT devices.
- Retail and channel for external SSDs and removable media.

## Role in the memory supply chain
SanDisk, as part of Western Digital, is primarily a product and branding layer that integrates NAND memory (sourced from wafer suppliers or WD's supply agreements), controller firmware, and mechanical packaging into finished consumer and OEM products. The company sits downstream from raw NAND wafer manufacturers and upstream from retail channels and OEM customers.

Key touchpoints:
- NAND sourcing: works with wafer foundries and NAND suppliers for die supply (either via WD's vertical arrangements or third-party suppliers).
- Controller and firmware: integrates controllers and firmware optimized for SanDisk product lines (client SSD controllers, removable-media controllers).
- Manufacturing & assembly: coordinates assembly, testing, and qualification with contract manufacturers and WD-owned facilities.

## Typical use-cases and examples
- A camera manufacturer sources SanDisk-branded microSD cards for retail distribution.
- A laptop OEM integrates a SanDisk NVMe SSD into consumer SKUs (branded or white-labeled).
- An embedded systems vendor purchases SanDisk eMMC modules for a low-power IoT device.

## Quick facts
- Typical HQ / origin: SanDisk originated in the United States; brand now under Western Digital.
- Parent company: Western Digital (acquired 2016).
- Typical product categories: removable flash (SD/USB), embedded flash (eMMC/UFS), client SSDs.
